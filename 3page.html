Overview of the Ottoman Empire

The Ottoman Empire was one of the longest-lasting and most powerful empires in history. Founded around 1299 by Osman I in northwestern Anatolia, it grew into a transcontinental empire that controlled large parts of Southeast Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

At its peak during the 16th and 17th centuries under sultans like Suleiman the Magnificent, the empire stretched from Hungary in the north to Yemen in the south, and from Algeria in the west to Iraq and the Persian Gulf in the east.

The Ottomans were known for their strong military (especially the Janissaries), advanced administration, tolerance toward different religions (millet system), magnificent architecture (Sinan’s mosques), and rich cultural life blending Turkish, Persian, Arabic, and Byzantine influences.

The empire officially ended in 1922 after World War I, when the sultanate was abolished, and the modern Republic of Turkey was founded in 1923 under Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.

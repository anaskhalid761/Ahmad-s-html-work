Introduction

Mehmed II, known as Fatih ("the Conqueror"), was the seventh sultan of the Ottoman Empire. He ruled from 1444-1446 and then again from 1451-1481. He is most famous for conquering Constantinople in 1453 at the age of 21, ending the Byzantine Empire after more than 1,100 years and transforming the city into the new Ottoman capital â€” Istanbul.

Mehmed was a brilliant military strategist, scholar, poet, and patron of the arts. He spoke several languages and was deeply interested in science, philosophy, and religion.

His Reign - Major Events

  1. First accession to the throne (1444-1446) at age 12
  2. Second and permanent reign begins in 1451
  3. Conquest of Constantinople - May 29, 1453
  4. Conquest of Serbia (1459), Morea (1460), Bosnia (1463)
  5. Conquests in Anatolia and the Black Sea region (Trabzon 1461, Genoese colonies)
  6. Campaigns against Wallachia, Moldavia, and the Knights of Rhodes
  7. Death in 1481 during a campaign (possibly poisoned)
